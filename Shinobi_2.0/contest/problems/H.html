Problem H — The Shinobi Archive

Time Limit: 1.00 s
Memory Limit: 512 MB
Within the deepest chambers of the ISIMS Ninja Clan headquarters, generations of battle data are encoded onto ancient scrolls. These scrolls contain the power levels of every warrior who has ever defended the institute. During the high-stakes SHINOBI 2.0 event, the Grandmasters frequently need to calculate the collective energy of a specific unit of ninjas to deploy them effectively against incoming threats.

The archive is represented as an array of n integers, where each integer xi is the energy signature of a shinobi at position i. However, the battlefield is chaotic. You will receive q urgent intelligence requests from the field commanders. Each request defines a range [a, b] and demands the total sum of power levels in that specific section of the archive.

In a real-world skirmish, speed is the only currency. If your calculation takes too long, the window of opportunity closes. While a novice might try to manually sum the values for every request, a True Ninja understands that repeated work is the enemy of efficiency.

Input

The first input line contains two integers n and q: the total number of shinobi power levels and the number of intelligence queries to be processed.

The second line contains n integers x1, x2, ..., xn: the individual power level of each ninja in the archive.

Finally, the input contains q lines. Each line provides two integers a and b (1-indexed), defining the boundaries of the unit currently under observation.

Output

For each query, output a single integer: the total power level of the shinobi unit in the range [a, b].

Constraints

  • 1 ≤ n, q ≤ 2 · 105
  • 1 ≤ xi ≤ 109
  • 1 ≤ a ≤ b ≤ n

Example

Input 
8 4
3 2 4 5 1 1 5 3
2 4
5 6
1 8
3 3
Output 
11
2
24
4
