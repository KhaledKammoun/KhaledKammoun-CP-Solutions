Problem E — Kam Anta Kabir ya Bakhano

Time Limit: 1.00 s
Memory Limit: 512 MB
Officer Azzouz is chasing the famous sugar addict Bakhano. Bakhano hid his stash in a long row of n warehouses. Each warehouse has a different height.

Azzouz is looking at a map of the n warehouses from left to right. The warehouses are numbered from 1 to n. Each warehouse i has a height of xi.

To move between the rooftops easily, Azzouz uses a grappling hook. From his current warehouse, he will throw his hook to the nearest warehouse on his left that is strictly shorter than his current warehouse.

Your task is to help Azzouz. For every warehouse position, find the index of the nearest shorter warehouse to its left.

Input

The first line contains an integer n: the total number of warehouses.

The second line contains n integers x1, x2, ..., xn: the heights of the warehouses from left to right.

Output

Print n integers separated by spaces. For each warehouse, print the index of the nearest shorter warehouse to its left. If there is no shorter warehouse to the left, print 0.

Constraints

  • 1 ≤ n ≤ 2 ċ 105
  • 1 ≤ xi ≤ 109

Example

Input 
8
2 5 1 4 8 3 2 5
Output 
0 1 0 3 4 3 3 7
Note:
Let's explain the example test case with the heights: 2 5 1 4 8 3 2 5.
  • Warehouse 1 (height 2): No warehouse to the left. Answer is 0.
  • Warehouse 2 (height 5): Warehouse 1 (height 2) is shorter. Answer is 1.
  • Warehouse 3 (height 1): No shorter warehouse to the left. Answer is 0.
  • ...
  • Warehouse 6 (height 3): We look to the left. Warehouse 5 (height 8) and Warehouse 4 (height 4) are too tall. Warehouse 3 (height 1) is shorter. Answer is 3.
Judge Formatting Warning:
The grading system uses a strict string comparison. You must print exactly one space after every number, including the last one.
  • For C++: A standard loop like cout << ans << " "; is perfect. Do not try to remove the final space.
  • For Python: Force the trailing space by using: print(*res, end=" \n").
